Unearthing Ancient Mysteries: Research for "The Egyptian Codex"

February 28, 2026

Writing "The Egyptian Codex" took me on a journey through ancient Egyptian history, exploring the reign of Pharaoh Nectanebo II and the mysterious codices that may have preserved lost knowledge.

The Last Native Pharaoh

Nectanebo II ruled Egypt from 359 to 343 BCE, serving as the last native Pharaoh before the Macedonian conquest. His reign represented the final flowering of indigenous Egyptian kingship, and his story is largely untold in popular fiction.

What fascinated me most was Nectanebo's reputation as a magician and his supposed flight to Macedonia—where he allegedly mentored young Alexander the Great. This connection between Egypt's last king and history's most famous conqueror became the foundation for my novel.

The Lost Codices

Ancient codices were more than books—they were vessels of power, containing secrets that kingdoms would kill to possess. In "The Egyptian Codex," I explore what might have happened if certain codices had been hidden away, waiting to be rediscovered.

The challenge was blending historical accuracy with fictional intrigue while honoring the cultural significance of these artifacts. I consulted archaeological records, studied temple inscriptions, and researched the spiritual practices of late-period Egypt.

Ancient Wisdom Today

This research deepened my appreciation for how ancient wisdom continues to influence our understanding of faith and heritage. The Egyptians developed sophisticated systems of medicine, astronomy, and spirituality that we are only beginning to understand.

"The Egyptian Codex" is my love letter to this ancient civilization—an attempt to bring its forgotten stories back to life in a way that honors the past while entertaining modern readers.
